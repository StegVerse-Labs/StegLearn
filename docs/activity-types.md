# Activity Types

StegLearn supports many kinds of learning activity.

The system must not reduce education to reading, worksheets, lectures, quizzes, or screen tasks.

## Purpose

Activity types help the system record what the learner did and how it may count as evidence.

An activity type is not a ranking.

It is a description of the learning form.

## Question

The learner asks, refines, compares, or revisits a question.

## Build

The learner creates a physical or digital artifact.

Examples: LEGO structure, model, craft, Minecraft structure, map, mechanism, diagram.

## Observation

The learner observes an object, event, pattern, place, animal, sky condition, social situation, or process.

## Experiment

The learner makes a prediction, tries something, observes the result, and explains what changed.

## Story

The learner tells, writes, draws, acts, or records a narrative.

## Drawing

The learner uses visual expression to represent an idea, memory, plan, observation, or imagined system.

## Video

The learner frames, records, edits, explains, or presents reality through video.

## Game Design

The learner creates or modifies a rule system, level, map, scenario, or multiplayer experience.

## Reading

The learner reads or listens to text and then explains, applies, questions, retells, or connects it.

## Outdoor Walk

The learner notices, collects, compares, maps, counts, narrates, or reflects during outdoor movement.

## Conversation

The learner develops an idea through dialogue.

## Music and Mood

The learner connects sound, pacing, emotion, memory, movement, or visual atmosphere.

## Logic Puzzle

The learner reasons through constraints, sequences, comparisons, or rules.

## Physical Challenge

The learner practices movement, coordination, stamina, balance, strength, safety, or body awareness.

## Care Task

The learner helps care for a person, animal, object, space, plant, or shared responsibility.

## Code

The learner writes, modifies, tests, or explains instructions that produce behavior in a system.

## Research

The learner gathers information, compares sources, asks follow-up questions, and explains what seems reliable.

## Reflection

The learner considers what happened, what changed, what was hard, what mattered, or what should happen next.

## V1 Requirement

Each receipt may contain one primary activity type and multiple secondary activity types.

## Non-Capture Rule

Repeated activity types may show interests or tendencies, but must not become fixed identity labels.
