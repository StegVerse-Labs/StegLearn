# Learner Loop

The StegLearn learner loop is the smallest complete unit of learning evidence.

It begins with learner attention and ends with a reviewed receipt.

## Core Loop

```text
Wonder
→ Build or Observe
→ Explain
→ Review
→ Receipt
→ Portfolio
```

## 1. Wonder

The learner begins with a question, observation, frustration, desire, or idea.

Examples:

- Why does sleep feel instant?
- Does the room still exist when I am not looking at it?
- What is the opposite of a black hole?
- Can I build a game other people will play?
- Why did my tower fall?

## 2. Build or Observe

The learner interacts with the world.

This may happen on screen, off screen, outdoors, through conversation, through play, or through physical construction.

The screen should organize the encounter, not replace it.

## 3. Explain

The learner explains what they noticed, made, changed, expected, misunderstood, or wants to try next.

Explanation is treated as a primary learning artifact.

Early learners may explain through speech, gesture, drawing, play, or parent-transcribed language.

## 4. Review

A parent or steward reviews the learning event.

The review may add context, subject mappings, safety notes, developmental notes, questions to revisit, related artifacts, or homeschool compliance references.

## 5. Receipt

The system generates a structured receipt.

The receipt does not claim that the learner mastered a subject unless the evidence supports that claim.

The receipt records what happened, what was expressed, and what can be reasonably inferred from the evidence.

## 6. Portfolio

Receipts accumulate over time into a portfolio.

The portfolio should show growth across questions, artifacts, explanations, revisions, and responsibilities.

## Design Rule

The loop must stay small enough to use daily.

A single receipt should be able to capture a short observation, a longer build, or a multi-day project.

## Failure Modes to Avoid

### Passive Screen Capture

The system should not treat watching content as sufficient evidence unless the learner also explains, applies, questions, builds, or reflects.

### False Mastery

A correct answer does not automatically prove understanding.

### Forced Output

The learner should not be forced to produce a polished artifact every time.

Some learning receipts may document confusion, failed experiments, partial explanations, or questions that remain open.

### Identity Capture

The system should not label a learner as a fixed type based on early patterns.

Patterns may guide scaffolding, but they must not become destiny.
